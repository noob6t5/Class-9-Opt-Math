Understanding Limits & Continuity

Class 9 Optional Mathematics Interactive Module

🎯 What is a Limit?

A limit answers: "Where is the function heading?"

🚗 Road Analogy for Limit:

You only care where the road is heading as you approach a location.
Even if there's a missing brick exactly at that location, you can still tell where the road is going.

The limit can exist even if the function is undefined at that exact point.

📍 Left Hand Limit (LHL) & Right Hand Limit (RHL)

LHL (👈 From Left): What value does \(f(x)\) approach as \(x\) gets closer to 3 from values less than 3?

RHL (👉 From Right): What value does \(f(x)\) approach as \(x\) gets closer to 3 from values greater than 3?

When LHL = RHL, we say the overall limit exists.

🔄 Continuity (The Grand Test)

🚧 Road Analogy for Continuity:

Now you care whether the road is actually complete at that location.
If there's a hole, jump, or break, continuity fails.

For a function to be continuous at a point \(x = a\), it must pass 3 strict conditions:

  1. The overall limit must exist: \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) \text{ exists (i.e., LHL = RHL)}\)
  2. The function must be defined at that point: \(f(a) \text{ must exist}\)
  3. The limit and the exact value must match perfectly: \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)\)
